What is Carpentry and Joinery?

Carpentry and joinery are skilled trades within the woodworking industry that focus on the design, construction, and installation of wooden structures and products. These crafts combine technical knowledge, precision, and creativity to transform raw timber into functional and durable components used in buildings, furniture, and interior fittings.

Carpentry mainly involves structural work carried out on construction sites. Carpenters are responsible for building and installing frameworks, roofing structures, flooring systems, staircases, and other wooden parts of buildings. Their work ensures that structures are strong, safe, and properly aligned.

Joinery: Precision & Fine Craftsmanship

Joinery focuses on the detailed crafting of wooden components, typically in a controlled workshop environment. Joiners create high-quality, precise items such as doors, window frames, cabinets, tables, chairs, staircases, and custom furniture. This trade demands exceptional accuracy, fine measurements, and deep knowledge of wood behavior (grain, moisture, expansion).

Unlike carpentry's larger structural focus, joinery emphasizes beautiful, tight joints (mortise & tenon, dovetails, dowels, biscuits) and smooth finishes. Joiners often work with hardwoods and apply techniques like hand-planing, chiseling, and sanding to achieve professional results. Many joiners also handle decorative elements, moldings, and built-in fittings for homes and offices.

Both trades complement each other — carpentry builds the skeleton of a building, while joinery adds the refined, visible details that make spaces functional and elegant.
