Core Fundamental Subjects

Our course follows Malawi TEVET standards, combining hands-on carpentry/joinery with these mandatory fundamental subjects. Each module builds essential knowledge for success in the trade — from safety to business skills. Click each for more details (separate pages coming soon).

1. Technology

Introduction to modern tools, machinery, and basic technology used in woodworking — including power tools, measuring devices, and emerging tech in construction.

Learn More →

2. Science

Basic principles of materials science, wood properties (strength, moisture, durability), adhesives, and how science applies to timber behavior and construction stability.

Learn More →

3. Numeracy / Mathematics

Practical calculations for measurements, angles, quantities, costing materials, scaling drawings, and estimating project needs — essential for accurate carpentry work.

Learn More →

4. Communication Graphics / Technical Drawing

Reading and creating blueprints, plans, elevations, sections, isometric views, and sketches — key for understanding designs and communicating on site or in the workshop.

Learn More →

5. Communication

Effective verbal/written communication, report writing, client interaction, teamwork on sites, and presenting your work — vital for jobs and running your own business.

Learn More →

6. Entrepreneurship

Starting and managing a small carpentry workshop, costing jobs, finding clients, basic business planning, marketing your services, and turning skills into income in Malawi.

Learn More →

7. Occupational Safety and Health (OSH)

Workshop/site safety rules, PPE use, hazard identification, first aid, safe tool handling, and preventing accidents — mandatory for all TEVET trades and real-world work.

Learn More →

These fundamentals complement our main carpentry & joinery practical training (workshop operations, site work, joints, furniture, roofing, etc.).
