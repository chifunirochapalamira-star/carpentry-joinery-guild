Meaning of the Module

This module teaches how to start and run a viable (successful) business. It takes you from identifying real opportunities in carpentry to actually launching and managing a profitable workshop or site-based business.

Learning Outcomes

  • 1. Starting a viable business
  • 2. Running a profitable business

These include identifying business opportunities, planning, managing resources, costing jobs, marketing your work, and ensuring you make real profit every month.

Key Steps to Start a Profitable Carpentry Business

1. Identify Business Opportunities

In Malawi look for demand in:
• Doors & window frames for new houses
• School desks, beds & tables
• Coffins & funeral furniture
• Kitchen cabinets & wardrobes
• Roofing trusses for construction sites

2. Prepare a Simple Business Plan

Write down:
• What you will make and sell
• Who your customers are (builders, schools, families)
• How much money you need to start
• How you will make profit

3. Cost Your Products Correctly

Example for a standard bed:
• Timber = K85,000
• Nails & screws = K8,000
• Labour = K25,000
• Transport = K7,000
Total cost = K125,000
Sell at K180,000 → Profit = K55,000

4. Manage Resources & Money

• Buy quality timber in bulk to save money
• Keep simple records of every sale and expense
• Save 20% of profit for tools and emergencies
• Use mobile money (Airtel Money / TNM Mpamba) for payments

5. Market Your Services in Malawi

• Put up signboards at your workshop
• Take photos of finished work and post on WhatsApp groups
• Visit construction sites and offer doors/frames
• Join local carpenters associations for referrals

6. Ensure Profit Every Month

• Charge enough to cover costs + your living expenses
• Finish jobs on time to get paid quickly
• Reduce waste (measure twice, cut once!)
• Reinvest profit to buy better tools

Practical Tips for Carpenters in Malawi

  • Start small — make 2-3 beds or 5 doors first and sell them locally
  • Register your business with the Malawi Revenue Authority (MRA) for legitimacy
  • Keep a simple notebook or phone app for daily sales and expenses
  • Build relationships with hardware shops and builders for steady orders
  • Always deliver quality work — happy customers bring more customers

After finishing Module 2 you will know exactly how to turn your carpentry skills into a real business that pays your bills and grows every year.
